Family Medicine

The Family Medicine clerkship at the clinical campus administered by GMCC is a 6-week program during which students undertake clinical training at recognized teaching hospitals in California.

Students rotate with dedicated clinical faculty at GMCC host hospitals, clinics, and private practices.

Students will rotate with primary care faculty who are solely dedicated to community and family medicine.

Additionally, students will have the opportunity to participate in the most challenging and complex Family Medicine cases.

"My attending’s practice was extremely busy and demanding, which exposes you to a high volume of work for someone whowants to go into primary practice."

Why should you do your Family Medicine clerkship at GMCC?

  • Student/Faculty ratio is as low as 2:1
  • Experience patient care in both inpatient and ambulatory settings
  • Rotate at teaching hospital host sites with prestigious academic affiliations
  • Exposure to a wide range of cases through, Urgent/Emergent community-based sites, and various hospital units
  • Weekly didactic lectures focused on preparing students to succeed on USMLE Step 2
  • Dedicated on-site administration for clinical student affairs

Psychiatry

The Psychiatry clerkship at the clinical campus administered by GMCC is a 6-week program during which students undertake clinical training at recognized teaching hospitals in California.

Students rotate with dedicated clinical faculty at GMCC host hospitals, clinics, and private practices.

Each student experiences four weeks of training with an outpatient attending physician and two weeks of training exclusively with a mix of inpatient and outpatient.

Clinical Simulations

Simulation Rooms

Genesis Medicus Clinical Campus offers students a place to develop their clinical skills, allowing them to experience real-world scenarios with realistic patients.

Cardionics Simscope

The SimScope is the foundational device of our wearable simulators system.

Programmed from a large library of cardiac, respiratory, bowel, and bruit sounds, the SimScope allows students the ability to auscultate a Standardized Patient with the sounds appropriate for the scenario. The upgraded WiFi model allows parameters to be altered on the fly through the system’s own WiFi network.

Gaumard® Simulators

COMING SOON

Gaumard® Scientific company has designed, manufactured, and marketed simulators for health care education for more than 60 years. Users worldwide - the military, emergency medical services, major teaching hospitals, and nursing schools - recognize Gaumard products for their innovation in simulation in the pre-hospital, obstetrics and gynecology, surgical, and nursing care segments.

Victoria S2200

VICTORIA® is capable of simulating high-risk, low-frequency deliveries, and nonpostpartum care emergencies for OB/GYN and med-surg training.

Interactive Eyes

- Visually track objects

- Visually present signs of: stroke, stress, head trauma, drug use, other diseases

Supports Real-life Monitoring Equipment:

- EKG

- Caponography

- Defibrillator

- Pulse Oximeter

Tory S2210

TORY® offers true-to-life physical and physiological attributes, wireless mobility, and ease-of-use designed to simulate lifelike clinical cases for every stage in neonatal care. Whether training in a simulation center, in situ, or in transit, TORY brings neonatal simulation closer to real-life than ever before.

Hal S5301

From emergency care to ICU and med-surg training, HAL is engineered to fulfill educational objectives across clinical disciplines and blur the lines between simulation and real life. New conversational speech, lifelike motor movement, next-gen simulated physiology, UNI® 3, and many more industry-first capabilities usher in the next revolutionary leap in simulation.

Pediatric Hal S2225

Pediatric HAL® is the world's most advanced pediatric patient simulator and the first capable of simulating lifelike emotions through dynamic facial expressions, movement, and speech. HAL is designed to help providers of all levels develop the specialized skills needed to effectively communicate, diagnose, and treat young patients in nearly all clinical areas.
